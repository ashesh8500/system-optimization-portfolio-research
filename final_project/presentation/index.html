How to read the study

From static allocation to layered control

Research objective

The study starts from mean-variance allocation, then asks what happens when the portfolio system also optimizes the upstream opportunity set, the active screen, the rebalancing controller, and the model-selection rule.

Presentation design

This website moves as a story: why static Markowitz is not enough → what layers were optimized → how the walk-forward experiment was run → what changed across universe breadth → why learned routing is informative but not yet dominant.

Research question

Where should optimization be applied?

Classical portfolio optimization solves for weights after a universe is already chosen. The project asks whether that is the right abstraction.

Research flow log

How the study was performed

Proposal: formulate signal-based allocation as constrained optimization with possible game-theoretic extensions.
Mathematical reframing: model allocation as a sequential control problem with state, action, turnover cost, and finite-horizon reward.
Empirical substrate: construct chronological walk-forward ledgers so every tried screen/controller arm is recorded, not only the selected winner.
Layer expansion: scale from pilot baskets into 100/250/500 liquid-equity candidate scopes and evaluate a 60-arm factorial matrix.
Diagnostics: add ablations, cost sensitivity, winner-gap checks, and a policy-gradient routing experiment over interpretable arms.

Why this is still useful

The learned router acts over screen/controller arms, so its actions remain interpretable. Its non-dominance is an empirical finding: in a finite-sample setting, a transparent recency rule can be a very strong optimizer.

Threats to validity

The study is evidence for a mechanism, not a final trading system

Survivorship bias

Candidate universes approximate investable historical sets; point-in-time membership is the highest-priority improvement.

Finite folds

Top-250 and Top-500 have fewer independent walk-forward splits, which widens uncertainty around winner identity.

Model-selection bias

Each universe selects from 20 arms, so winner gaps must be interpreted with multiple-comparison caution.

Cost realism

A proportional cost is a controlled diagnostic, not a full market-impact or liquidity model.

Router sample size

The learned router has too few episodes for a deployable superiority claim.

Next study

Compute universe descriptors, rebuild point-in-time sets, and evaluate contextual routing against strict baselines.

Literature context

The project extends a known chain of portfolio optimization ideas

Classical baseline

  • Markowitz (1952): mean-variance allocation frames portfolio choice as risk-return optimization.
  • Transaction-cost portfolio optimization: linear/proportional costs make turnover a first-class part of the objective, not an afterthought.

Sequential and adaptive view

  • Cover (1991): online portfolio selection evaluates policies through nonanticipating sequential wealth updates.
  • Reinforcement-learning portfolio studies: policy methods can route or rebalance adaptively, but must be judged against simple online baselines.

This study's contribution is not another isolated backtest; it is the decomposition of the allocation system into universe, screen, weighting, cost, and routing layers with each layer measured out of sample.

Conclusion

Three things to remember

1

Portfolio allocation is a layered control system. Universe construction, screening, weighting, turnover, and routing are all optimization decisions.

2

The binding layer changes with breadth. Top-100 favors momentum plus equal weight; Top-250 favors low-volatility filtering; Top-500 rewards volatility-adjusted momentum plus active ranking.

3

Complexity must beat transparent baselines. A learned router is interesting because it learns structure, but it is not practically superior until it beats trailing-window routing across enough episodes.

The research moves from “which portfolio wins?” to “which layer should be optimized under this market structure?”
